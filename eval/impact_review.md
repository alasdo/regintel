# Impact Analysis Review Rubric

Review each selected impact analysis on a 1-5 scale.

## Rubric

### 1. Accuracy
Does the summary correctly describe what changed?

- 1 = clearly wrong
- 3 = partly right, but important errors or omissions
- 5 = accurate and well grounded

### 2. Specificity
Are affected functions and processes specific and plausible?

- 1 = generic or irrelevant
- 3 = partly useful, partly generic
- 5 = specific and operationally plausible

### 3. Actionability
Could a QA or regulatory professional act on the recommended action?

- 1 = not actionable
- 3 = partially actionable
- 5 = clear, practical next step

### 4. Citation quality
Are citations valid and relevant?

- 1 = invalid or weak
- 3 = mostly valid but not very helpful
- 5 = valid and clearly supportive

### 5. Appropriate confidence
Is confidence calibrated appropriately?

- 1 = clearly overconfident or underconfident
- 3 = somewhat off
- 5 = well calibrated

## Failure patterns to watch for

- removed section interpreted as eliminated obligation
- generic affected processes added by default
- action recommendations too vague
- citations valid but weakly relevant
- confidence too high when rulemaking context is ambiguous
